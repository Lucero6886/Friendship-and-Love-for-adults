Adult Speaking Lesson

Friendship & Love ❤️

Navigating human connections through English

Learning Objectives

  • Acquire advanced vocabulary to describe deep relationships.
  • Discuss complex relationship dynamics with structural idea support.
  • Critique and express opinions on modern dating and love trends.
1

Warm-up

Answer these questions with your partner or yourself:

1. What makes someone a "true" friend? 🤝

"In my view, a true friend is someone who stays by your side through thick and thin, offering support without judgment."

2. Do you believe in love at first sight? ✨

"To be honest, I think it's usually just an initial infatuation. True love takes time to develop as you get to know a person's character."

3. Is it easier to make friends as a child or an adult? 👶/🧔

"As an adult, it's more challenging because we have more responsibilities and are often more cautious about who we trust."
2

Vocabulary Hub

Soulmate

/ˈsoʊlmeɪt/

Someone perfectly suited to another person.

"Many people spend their entire lives searching for their soulmate."

Confidant

/ˈkɑːnfɪdænt/

A person you trust with your private secrets.

"My sister has always been my closest confidant."

Acquaintance

/əˈkweɪntəns/

Someone you know slightly, but not a close friend.

"He is just a business acquaintance I met at a conference."

Amiable

/ˈeɪmiəbl/

Friendly, pleasant, and easy to like.

"The meeting was very amiable, and we reached an agreement quickly."

Main Speaking Practice

Easy

1. How did you meet your best friend?

• Met at school/university
• Through a mutual friend
• At a previous job
• Shared hobby or club

Short: "We met at university while we were both studying for our final exams in the library."

Extended: "Actually, it's quite a funny story. We met through a mutual friend at a birthday party ten years ago. We immediately bonded over our shared love for jazz music and have been inseparable ever since."

Medium

5. Is it easy to make friends as an adult?

• Harder due to work-life balance
• People are more cautious
• Less opportunities than school
• Social media helps

Short: "In my opinion, it's much harder because most adults are busy with their careers and families."

Extended: "From my perspective, it’s significantly more difficult. When we were younger, school provided a natural environment for social interaction. As adults, we have to be more proactive, like joining clubs or networking events, to meet like-minded people."

Opinion

8. Can men and women be "just friends"?

• Depends on boundaries
• Platonic love is real
• Society's outdated view
• Risk of unrequited feelings

Short: "Yes, I believe platonic relationships are totally possible as long as there is respect."

Extended: "I’m of the mind that they can, though it requires maturity. I have several close friends of the opposite gender, and our bond is purely intellectual and emotional. However, the key is having clear boundaries from the very beginning."

Real-life Role-play

Scenario: Reconnecting with an old friend

You haven't spoken to a close friend in 2 years after a small argument. You meet at a cafe to "bury the hatchet" (make peace).

Student A (Initiator)
Start the conversation. Express that you missed the friendship.
Student B (Cautious)
Be a bit guarded but willing to listen. Ask how their life has been.
• "It’s been a while, hasn't it?"
• "I’ve been doing a lot of thinking lately..."
• "I really value our bond and I'm sorry about..."
• "How's your family/work been going?"
